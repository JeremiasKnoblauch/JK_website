About me

I am a postgraduate researcher working at the boundary of computer science and statistics within the Oxford-Warwick Statistics Programme (OxWaSP). I am jointly supervised by Theodoros Damoulas and Chenlei Leng. My interests are focused on scalable spatio-temporal inference procedures for data generating mechanisms in high dimensions that are ill-behaved or difficult to describe. This encompasses modelling and inference for non-stationary data streams whose behaviours change across time as well as space. My research is part of the Clean Air London project at the Turing Institute to support London’s Major’s office with data-driven policy. More information can be found in my (outdated) CV.
